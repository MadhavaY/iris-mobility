Who we are

A company started at the scene of a crash

Iris Mobility was founded in Richardson, Texas by three friends who witnessed a pedestrian crash firsthand — and couldn't stop asking why nothing had seen it coming. The patterns were there: the near-misses, the speeding, the drivers who never yielded. Nobody was watching.

"Using data to prevent crashes and protect lives — that's what we want to do for the communities of America."

Today, that conviction powers two products built on one perception engine: a traffic intelligence platform helping cities reach Vision Zero, and a mobile surveillance trailer protecting the places fixed cameras can't. We're backed by Startup Runway and already working with cities across Texas and beyond.

Safety before surveillance

We build to prevent harm — crashes, theft, intrusions — not to punish people. Our city product is deliberately non-punitive: no tickets, no fines.

Perception with judgment

Our cameras don't just record. On-device AI classifies what it sees, reasons about what matters, and acts in seconds — day or night.

Evidence over footage

Hours of unwatched video help nobody. We deliver structured records, reports, and exports that engineers, insurers, and courts can actually use.

Agentic AI

It doesn't record intruders. It talks them down.

When Sentinel classifies a person on your site at 2 AM, it doesn't wait for a human to check a feed. The onboard AI describes what it sees, addresses the intruder out loud by their appearance and position, fires the strobe, and decides whether to escalate — all within seconds.

Every event ends the same way: a time-stamped clip, a structured log entry, and a site that's still intact.
